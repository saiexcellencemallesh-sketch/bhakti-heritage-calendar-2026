February

Sant Tulsidas

The Turning Point

Before his sainthood, Tulsidas was consumed by intense attachment to his wife, Ratnavali. One night, he crossed a flooded river using a corpse as a raft just to see her. Ratnavali, seeing his obsession, delivered a spiritual shock:

"If you loved the Divine with the same intensity you feel for this body of flesh and bone, you would be realized in an instant."

These piercing words redirected his passion into an eternal love for Sri Rama, leading to the creation of the Ramcharitmanas.

March

Goda Devi (Andal)

Andal (8th Century) is the only female among the twelve Alvar saints of South India. Found as a baby under a Tulsi plant, she grew up breathing the air of devotion.

She is celebrated as 'Chudikudutha Sudarkodi'â€”the bright woman who gave the Lord the garland she had first worn herself. Her love was a sovereign claim over the Divine.

Tiruppavai & Nachiar Tirumozhi

The Tiruppavai is a call to awaken the soul. Her second work, Nachiar Tirumozhi, expresses her intense longing for Lord Ranganatha, including her dream wedding.
