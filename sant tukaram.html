Sant Tukaram Maharaj

A revolutionary 17th-century Marathi saint-poet who brought spiritual wisdom to the masses through his Abhangs. A devotee of Lord Vitthala, he famously chose the "eternal wealth" of devotion over his worldly profession.

Goswami Tulsidas Ji

A 16th-century saint and poet whose devotion to Lord Rama changed Indian literature. He composed the Ramcharitmanas, making the Ramayana accessible to the common man in the Awadhi language.

Legacy: Legend says Lord Hanuman guided him to the darshan of Sri Rama at Chitrakoot.

Andal (Goda Devi)

The only female among the twelve Alvar saints of South India (8th Century). Found under a Tulsi plant, she is celebrated as 'Chudikudutha Sudarkodi'—the one who wore the garland before offering it to the Lord.
